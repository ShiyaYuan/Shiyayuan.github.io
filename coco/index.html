    Make It A Party Wherever You Go [publication]

    Make It A Party Wherever You Go is a semester-long project responding to a current issue about the lack of well-planned public space in Melbourne City. This project includes a self-made party table as an urban intervention, a printed publication and a website.

    This book represents my position with regards to the current issue about urban public space in Melbourne. It contains my point of view on the subject and documents the journey of the party table as I observe its interactions with people.

    Publication: 232 pages, 150mm x 230mm

    Feeling Healthy ON THE INSIDE [digital]

    Feeling Healthy On The Inside is a guide/resource advicing design students on how to stay emotionally healthy. This is a public Website for the public good.

    "When you’re working hard to sustain your creative vision and your livelihood, it can be hard to prioritize your own physical and mental health. But feeling physically strong and emotionally grounded is essential to maintaining focus, staying inspired, and doing your best work. So what does it take to feel good about your own body, mind, and overall wellbeing—and how can a creative practice feed into one’s health?" (The Creative Independent.)

    One thing I've learnt about maintaining my emotional health is that you have to view your problems from the right perspective. When you view things from the inside, you can see what the problem is hence able to solve it, but when you are on the outside, things don't appear as straight forward as they may be.

    Is Collingwood Getting Safer? [video]

    Is Collingwood Getting Safer? Is a short video representing my proposed use of a disused space in Collingwood @45 Keele St. (was a disused studio space).

    Through exploration and research of Collingwood and its people— residents, people who work there, students, etc. There seems to be debates on whether Collingwood is actually getting better or worse drawing attention to safety concerns.

    As a response to this issue, I propose to use the studio space as an installation of CCTV— turning the studio into a virtual alternate of an outdoor space in Collingwood, where people in the studio and people on the street can see each other through live footage on the TVs. As a result, people in the studio unconsciously familiarise themselves with the space on the street (through cctv), and people on the street can feel a sense of security seeing people in the studio are watching out for them. The aim is so that people can feel safer being and living in Collingwood.

    Is Collingwood Getting Safer? shows an inside view of the studio and a street view of Collingwood with a reflection of the CCTV camera watching the place.

    2019

    A Yellow Journal [publication] [digital]

    A Yellow Journal is a Print On Demand publication using only early web fonts like Arial and Times. This project also includes a website to promote the book.

    Based on an ezine from 1996 and supplemented with content from 2019, A Yellow Journal recontextualises the radical use of the internet during the late 1990s, and looks how our ways of using the internet have changed since then.

    Publication: 122 Pages, 130mm × 200mm

    Trapped Within [animation]

    Trapped Within is a frame-by-frame animation responding to the brief Sense of Place. 

    Sometimes I find myself trapped within a space of thought that I refer to as "fluffy" thoughts–– disturbing and depressing thoughts that find their way to feed on their host. These annoying thoughts in a way "tickle" your heart leading to feelings of suffocation. However, these feelings may go away (for a brief second) when the mind is being distracted by physical activities. 

    Trapped Within communicates the idea that some people get trapped within a world of anxiety and depression that only an act of distraction could part them from that world. However, shortly after the distracting activity has been performed, "fluffy" thoughts return and continue to feed on their host.

    2018

    GeoGraphics for Geoflags [digital]

    GeoGraphics for GeoFlag is a digital project that hopes to create a morphing flag (the GeoFlag) using graphics (GeoGraphics) created with HTML and CSS.

    This website is a hompage for all the GeoGraphics I have created for this project.

    As this planetary-wide megastructure dissolves borders and create a new global totality, what flag would be raised to claim its global nation? A flag that represents both human and non human agents working together, its hungry thirst for the global material resources it requires, the interconnected grid of planetary vision and the coupling users and computers.

    *This project was created in class with generous support from Uriah Gray at U-P.
